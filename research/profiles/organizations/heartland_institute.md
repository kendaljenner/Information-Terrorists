## History

### Founding and Early Years (1984-1990s)

The Heartland Institute was founded in 1984 by David H. Padden as a conservative and libertarian public policy think tank. Initially focused on Chicago-area issues, the organization expanded to national prominence in the 1990s.

### Climate Change Denial Focus (1997-Present)

**1997:** Joined the Cooler Heads Coalition, a group dedicated to denying climate change science.

**Key Activities:**
- Publishes materials questioning scientific consensus on climate change
- Organizes International Conferences on Climate Change (ICCC)
- Promotes climate skepticism through various media channels
- Maintains lists of "scientists" questioning global warming

### Notable Controversies

#### 2012 Documents Leak

Internal documents were leaked revealing:
- Fundraising strategies
- Donor information
- Plans to influence public education on climate change

**Impact:** Lost approximately $825,000 in funding (one-third of planned corporate fundraising) due to sponsor withdrawals.

#### 2012 Billboard Campaign

Controversial billboard campaign comparing climate believers to:
- Ted Kaczynski (Unabomber)
- Charles Manson
- Fidel Castro

**Result:** Widespread condemnation and loss of corporate sponsors.

## Policy Positions

### Climate Change

**Official Stance:**
- Rejects scientific consensus on anthropogenic climate change
- Claims climate change is not catastrophic
- Argues economic costs of mitigation exceed benefits
- Promotes climate change skepticism

**The New York Times** described Heartland as "the primary American organization pushing climate change skepticism."

**Quote from Naomi Klein (This Changes Everything):**
"Institute staff 'recognize that climate change is a profound threat to our economic and social systems and therefore deny its scientific reality.'"

### Other Policy Areas

- **Tobacco Regulation:** Opposes smoking restrictions as infringing on personal liberty
- **Healthcare:** Advocates for free-market healthcare solutions
- **Education:** Promotes school choice and voucher programs
- **Hydraulic Fracturing:** Supports fracking and fossil fuel development
- **Privatization:** Advocates for privatization of government services
