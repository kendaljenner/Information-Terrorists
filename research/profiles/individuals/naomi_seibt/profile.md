## Background

### Early Life and Education

Naomi Seibt was born on August 18, 2000, in Münster, North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany. She grew up in a politically engaged family environment. Her mother is a lawyer who has represented politicians associated with the far-right Alternative for Germany (AfD) party. Seibt has one sister, and the family continues to reside in Münster.

Seibt attended Bischöfliches Gymnasium St. Mauritz, a Catholic high school in Münster, graduating in September 2017. During her high school years, she demonstrated academic excellence, achieving first place in physics in the local regional competition of Jugend forscht ("Youth Researches") junior division, as well as second place in mathematics.

### Political Awakening

According to a February 19, 2020 interview with Die Weltwoche, Seibt's interest in politics began when she started attending political events with her mother in 2015. Initially, she was critical of the Christian Democratic Union (CDU) party. Her political trajectory would later shift significantly toward far-right ideologies.

At age 16, her poem on nationalism titled "Sometimes I Keep Silent" was published on David Berger's anti-Islamisation blog Philosophia Perennis as part of an AfD competition. This marked her early entry into far-right political discourse.

## Public Presence and Media Activities

### YouTube Career

Seibt began recording YouTube videos in May 2019, using her mobile phone to create content on topics ranging from migration to feminism to climate change. She describes herself as a "climate realist" rather than a climate denier, arguing that the latter term invokes Holocaust denialism.

**Notable Video Titles:**
- "Climate change – just hot air?"
- "Fierce without Feminism"
- "Message to the Media – HOW DARE YOU?"

As of February 2020, she had approximately 50,000 YouTube subscribers; by late May 2020, this had grown to about 86,700. Her following on X (formerly Twitter) surged significantly after she repeatedly referenced QAnon.

### Social Media Following

- **X (Twitter):** Approximately 370,000 followers (as of 2025)

## Major Public Appearances

### 2019 Climate and Energy Conference

On November 2, 2019, Seibt delivered a star-making address at the European Institute for Climate and Energy's (EIKE) annual "International Climate and Energy Conference" in Munich. The Süddeutsche Zeitung, one of Germany's largest daily newspapers, described her appearance by stating, "They have their own Greta. A climate change denier Greta."

In her speech, Seibt stated that before she started questioning feminism and "cultural socialism," she too was a "climate alarmist."

### Madrid Climate Reality Forum

On December 3, 2019, Seibt spoke as an invited guest at the Madrid "Climate Reality Forum," organized to rebut the United Nations' climate change warnings, while Greta Thunberg spoke at COP25 several miles away. She was the only woman invited to speak at an event traditionally dominated by older men.

### Heartland Institute Engagement

In February 2020, the Heartland Institute, a U.S. libertarian and climate skeptic think tank based in Chicago, hired Seibt as the face of its climate denial campaign. The Institute has previously lobbied on behalf of tobacco firms and supports fracking.

**Key Activities with Heartland:**
- Created promotional video titled "Naomi Seibt vs. Greta Thunberg: Whom Should We Trust?" (February 2020)
- Spoke at Heartland's International Conference on Climate Change at Caesars Palace, Las Vegas (Fall 2021), dubbed the "anti-Cop26"
- Developed the counter-message: "I don't want you to panic. I want you to think" (referencing Thunberg's "I want you to panic" statement at Davos 2019)

Her contract with Heartland formally ended in April 2020. Seibt claimed it was her decision not to renew, citing threats from Landesanstalt für Medien NRW to delete her YouTube channel on grounds that she was influencing German politics for an American think tank.

### Conservative Political Action Conference (CPAC)

Seibt has spoken at CPAC, the annual conference for conservative activists in America, addressing approximately one hundred conservatives about her views on climate change and politics.

## Connection to Alternative for Germany (AfD)

### Direct Affiliation

Naomi Seibt is an outspoken supporter of the Alternative for Germany (AfD) party. The AfD is Germany's largest opposition party and has been classified by German domestic intelligence as a "right-wing extremist" organization.

**AfD Key Characteristics:**
- Founded in April 2013
- Platform includes opposition to immigration (especially Muslim immigration), Islam, and the European Union
- Advocates for mass deportations, German nationalism, and völkisch nationalism (ethno-nationalism)
- Climate change denial as part of political campaign
- Russia-friendly stance
- Achieved 20.8% of vote in 2025 federal election (second place)

### Influence within AfD

Seibt's influence extends beyond mere support. She has been described as instrumental in connecting international figures to the AfD:

- **Elon Musk Connection:** Credited with introducing Elon Musk to the AfD. In June 2024, Seibt posted: "My name is Naomi Seibt and I'm voting AfD." Musk responded, asking about the party, which led to Seibt creating explanatory videos in English.
- **Alice Weidel Platform:** Through Seibt's connection with Musk, AfD leader Alice Weidel was able to secure a high-profile livestreamed conversation on X in January 2025.
- **Martin Sellner Connection:** Seibt acknowledges being a friend of Martin Sellner, leader of the ethno-nationalist Identitarian movement.

### Statements on AfD

Seibt has defended the AfD against extremist designations, stating she does not regard herself or the party as right-wing extremists. However, she has acknowledged some AfD politicians make "insensitive" comments while defending controversial statements by former AfD leader Alexander Gauland about the Holocaust.

## Asylum Application in the United States

### October 2025 Announcement

On October 28, 2025, Seibt announced she had applied for political asylum in the United States under Section 208 of the Immigration and Nationality Act.

### Stated Reasons for Asylum

- **Alleged Persecution:** Claims she is being persecuted in Germany for her political views
- **Death Threats:** States she receives death threats from Antifa
- **Intelligence Surveillance:** Claims German domestic intelligence (BfV) has been tracking her movements for years
- **Lack of Protection:** Alleged German police told her they "can't do anything" about threats unless she is "actually raped or killed"
- **Potential Imprisonment:** Claims she could face imprisonment if she returns to Germany for social media posts under Article 188 (extended under Angela Merkel)

### Support from U.S. Political Figures

- **Elon Musk:** Claims Musk has privately encouraged her asylum application, stating he is "terrified to go to Europe" due to high threat levels
- **Representative Anna Paulina Luna:** Florida Republican who met with Seibt and pledged to support her asylum application, stating Seibt has been targeted for "rejecting groupthink"
- **John Lott:** Publicly supported her asylum claim on social media

### Goal

Seibt stated her goal is to become an American citizen, saying: "This country has given me so much hope."
