## Background

### Origin Story

Rainer Winkler gained notoriety in the German-speaking internet not through planned content creation, but due to a massive cyberbullying scene that formed against him. What began as isolated actions evolved into a sustained, organized harassment campaign lasting over a decade.

### The "Drachengame"

**Terminology:**
- The harassment is euphemistically called the "Drachengame" (Dragon Game) by the perpetrators
- Participants call themselves "Hater" or "Anti-Fans"
- **NOT a game:** Real-world harassment, vandalism, and criminal activity

## Harassment Timeline and Tactics

### Early Phase (Pre-2014)

**Initial Conflicts:**
- Conflicts began before 2014
- Winkler continuously reacted to provocations, often escalating situations

**February 2014 - Critical Escalation:**
- Hater doxxed Winkler's sister and threatened her with distorted voice
- Winkler responded with video revealing his full address, challenging perpetrators to come to him
- This proved to be a catastrophic mistake, enabling years of real-world harassment

### Ongoing Harassment (2014-2024+)

#### 1. Hater Community Structure

**Platforms:**
- **Discord:** Multiple servers for coordination
- **Telegram:** Largest group has tens of thousands of members
- **YouTube:** Thousands of anti-Winkler videos (reactions, parodies, leaks)
- **Websites:** News-portal style sites reporting on "Drachengame" developments
- **Chronicles:** Meticulous archives of all Winkler and hater content

**Organizational Sophistication:**
- Well-organized community with defined structure
- Content creation (anti-Winkler videos)
- Documentation and archiving
- Real-world coordination for visits to his residence

#### 2. Real-World Impact

**Vandalism at Residence:**
- Daily disturbances at his Altschauerberg home
- Property damage (Sachbeschädigungen)
- Trespassing (Hausfriedensbruch)
- Violations during COVID-19 pandemic (Infection Protection Act violations)
- Insults and violent crimes (Gewaltdelikte)

**Police Response Burden:**
- **2022 Statement by Middle Franconian Police:**
  - "Hardly a day without visits from sometimes rioting haters"
  - Average: 5-10 police dispatches per day
  - Peak days: up to 15 dispatches daily
  - Significant strain on police resources
- **150+ criminal proceedings** against haters open at time of Winkler's appeal trial

**Aftermath of Relocation:**
- Even after Winkler moved away from Altschauerberg
- Continued visits by sightseers
- Ongoing crimes: property damage, trespassing
- Police: "Individual cases, but they keep popping up"

#### 3. Swatting and Emergency Service Abuse

**July 2015 - First Documented German Swatting:**
- 24-year-old made false emergency call
- Large-scale deployment: fire department, police, rescue services
- Result: 3.5 year prison sentence for perpetrator (2017)
- First swatting case in Germany with court verdict

**Ongoing Emergency Abuse:**
- Repeated false emergency calls targeting Winkler
- May 2022: False call caused major police deployment at Nuremberg hotel where Winkler was staying

#### 4. Economic Harassment

**Business Impact:**
- Companies doing business with Winkler targeted
- Restaurants, hotels, private individuals in contact with him harassed
- Tactics: hate mail, prank calls, unwanted pizza deliveries, fake package orders

**Online Reputation Damage:**
- Businesses suffer from bad online reviews
- Delivery services struggle with unpaid bills
- Anyone contacting Winkler attracts "an armada of anonymous internet trolls" (Journalist Julia Ruhnau)

**Income Paradox:**
- Despite harassment, reportedly earns over €2,000/month on YouTube
- Hate-watching drives clicks and ad revenue
- More videos = more clicks = more revenue
- Creates perverse incentive structure

### Media and Public Response

#### 2014 Media Incident

**Radio Z (Nuremberg):**
- Winkler announced interview in two videos
- Over 1,000 emails and threatening calls to the station
- Interview not broadcast due to "questionable information content"

#### Political Response

**Government Recognition:**
- Politicians acknowledged Drachenlord case as serious societal problem
- Debate about internet platform accountability
- Discussion of cyberbullying legislation

**2022 Notable Event:**
- 4,000 "Drachenlord-Haters" attempted to "storm" Altschauerberg village
- Mass gathering in rural Bavaria specifically to harass Winkler
- Required significant police presence

### Legal Proceedings

**Criminal Proceedings:**
- Over 150 open cases against haters
- Multiple convictions of hater community members
- Winkler himself also convicted in some incidents

**Winkler's Convictions:**
- Various charges related to his responses to harassment
- Legal system struggled to distinguish victim from perpetrator in complex dynamic
