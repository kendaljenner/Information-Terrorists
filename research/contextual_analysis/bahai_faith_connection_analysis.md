## 1. Baháʼí Faith Overview

### Historical Origins

**Founders:**
1. **The Báb (1819-1850)** - Seyyed ʻAlí Muḥammad Shírází
   - Declared himself "the Báb" (the Gate) in 1844
   - Claimed to be the promised Mahdi/Qa'im of Islam
   - Executed by Persian authorities in 1850
   - His remains are interred at the Shrine of the Báb in Haifa, Israel

2. **Baháʼu'lláh (1817-1892)** - Mírzá Ḥusayn-ʻAlí Núrí
   - Early follower of the Báb
   - Imprisoned in Síyáh-Chál dungeon (1852)
   - Announced claim of prophethood in 1863
   - Exiled through Baghdad → Constantinople → Adrianople → ʻAkká
   - Died in 1892 at Bahjí, near Acre (Israel)
   - Produced 18,000+ works (Arabic and Persian)

### Core Beliefs

**Key Principles:**
1. **Unity of God** - Single divine source for all religions
2. **Unity of Religion** - Progressive revelation through messengers
3. **Unity of Humanity** - One human family
4. **Independent Investigation of Truth** - No blind faith

**Social Principles (Progressive for 1911-1912):**
- Equality of men and women
- Elimination of racial prejudice
- Universal education
- World peace and collective security
- International court of arbitration
- Universal auxiliary language
- Abolition of extremes of wealth and poverty
- Harmony of science and religion
- Work as worship
- Justice as ruling principle
- Permanent universal peace

### Administrative Structure

**Leadership Succession:**
1. **Baháʼu'lláh** (1863-1892) - Founder
2. **ʻAbdu'l-Bahá** (1892-1921) - Son and appointed successor
3. **Shoghi Effendi** (1921-1957) - Guardian, appointed by ʻAbdu'l-Bahá
4. **Universal House of Justice** (1963-present) - Elected supreme governing body

**Governance:**
- No clergy
- Democratic elections
- Local Spiritual Assemblies (9 members)
- National Spiritual Assemblies
- Universal House of Justice (9 members, elected every 5 years)
- Located at Baháʼí World Centre, Haifa, Israel
